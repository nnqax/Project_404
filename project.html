Our Mission: Fighting Digital Deception

At [Project 404], our purpose is to empower individuals with the skills to see beyond digital illusions.

In today's cyber landscape:

  • Propaganda manipulates public opinion.
  • Fake news spreads faster than facts.
  • Bots amplify lies to look like truth.
  • Hackers and scammers exploit human trust.

To counter this, three disciplines stand at the core of our approach:

  • OSINT (Open Source Intelligence): Finding truth in public data, from social media trails to geospatial intelligence.
  • Digital Forensics: Preserving and analyzing digital evidence, exposing the hidden fingerprints left in files and systems.
  • Cryptography: Protecting and decoding information, breaking the ciphers that attackers use to conceal their plans.

Together, these fields give learners not only knowledge, but also the mindset of an investigator. Our purpose is to make these tools accessible, engaging, and practical — turning awareness into defense.

OSINT Fundamentals

Learn Open Source Intelligence techniques to uncover digital footprints

OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)

OSINT is about finding hidden truths in public spaces. By analyzing social media, open records, or geotags in photos, investigators can uncover valuable insights without ever touching private data.

👉 Example: A fake account may post a photo with location metadata still attached. OSINT tools can reveal where and when the photo was actually taken.

OSINT relies mainly on:
a) Private hacking tools
b) Publicly available data
c) Secret government databases
GEOINT is a branch of OSINT that focuses on:
a) Encryption
b) Location and mapping data
c) Social media bots

Cryptography

Protecting and decoding digital information

Cryptography

Cryptography protects digital information by turning it into unreadable code that only the right key can unlock. Attackers also use cryptography — hiding disinformation in coded messages or embedding secrets in files.

👉 Example: A propaganda group might encrypt their instructions so only insiders with the passphrase can read them. Investigators must break the code to reveal the plan.

Which type of cryptography uses a pair of keys (public & private)?
a) Symmetric cryptography
b) Asymmetric cryptography
c) Hash functions
Cryptography ensures that:
a) Only authorized people can read the message
b) Messages are spread faster
c) Files cannot be deleted

Digital Forensics

Uncovering and preserving digital evidence

Digital Forensics

Forensics is the science of uncovering and preserving digital evidence. From deleted files to hidden metadata, forensic experts follow the footprints attackers leave behind.

👉 Example: A fake news image may look normal, but its metadata shows it was created with editing software days before the "breaking event" it claims to depict.

Which of these can digital forensics uncover?
a) Deleted chat logs
b) Secret PINs of bank accounts
c) Handwritten notes
Why is forensics important in cybercrime?
a) It helps investigators trace and preserve digital evidence
b) It allows hackers to erase their tracks completely
c) It spreads fake news faster
