Our Mission: Fighting Digital Deception

In today's digital world, cyber threats have evolved beyond simple viruses to sophisticated campaigns of misinformation, automated bots, and coordinated phishing attacks.

Our platform transforms cybersecurity education into an engaging, interactive experience where you learn by doing. Through realistic scenarios and hands-on challenges, you'll develop the skills needed to:

  • Detect and analyze propaganda campaigns
  • Identify and track automated bot networks
  • Verify information using OSINT techniques
  • Understand cryptographic principles for data verification
  • Analyze digital evidence using forensic methods

Why This Matters

Every day, ordinary internet users face:

Propaganda Coordinated misinformation campaigns
Bot Networks Automated accounts manipulating public opinion
Phishing Deceptive attempts to steal sensitive information
Fake News Deliberately false information spread as news

OSINT Fundamentals

Learn Open Source Intelligence techniques to uncover digital footprints

Introduction to OSINT +

OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) involves collecting information from publicly available sources to identify threats, verify information, and track malicious activities.

Key concepts include social media intelligence gathering, image and metadata analysis, domain and IP investigation, and persona identification.

Practice Exercise:

Question: How would you verify the origin of a viral image claiming to show a current event?

Social Media Intelligence +

Learn how to gather intelligence from social media platforms while respecting privacy and terms of service.

Techniques include analyzing posting patterns, geolocation data, and network connections.

Practice Exercise:

Question: What are three indicators that a social media account might be part of a bot network?

Cryptography

Master encryption techniques to protect and verify digital information

Encryption Fundamentals +

Learn how cryptographic techniques protect information and verify authenticity.

Key concepts include symmetric vs asymmetric encryption, digital signatures, hash functions, and public key infrastructure.

Practice Exercise:

Question: Decode this Base64 message: V2VsY29tZSB0byBDeWJlckxlYXJu

Digital Signatures +

Understand how digital signatures work to verify the authenticity and integrity of digital messages or documents.

Learn about public key cryptography and how it enables secure digital communication.

Practice Exercise:

Question: What are the three properties that a digital signature must provide?

Digital Forensics

Learn to collect, preserve, and analyze digital evidence

Evidence Collection +

Learn to collect, preserve, and analyze digital evidence from various sources.

Key concepts include chain of custody procedures, file system analysis, network traffic examination, and timeline reconstruction.

Practice Exercise:

Question: What are the first steps when encountering a compromised system?

Memory Forensics +

Learn techniques for analyzing volatile memory to uncover running processes, network connections, and malware artifacts.

Memory analysis can reveal evidence that would be lost if a system is powered down.

Practice Exercise:

Question: Why is memory forensics important in incident response?

Case #77: The Propaganda Hunter

A message appears on your website's "Case Board" from a renowned Propaganda Hunter named Arthur Finch.

Case File: #77-ECHO // URGENT ASSISTANCE REQUIRED

"For months, I've been tracking 'Echo Corporation,' a shadowy firm that spreads disinformation. I've hit a wall. My informant inside is gone, but they left a digital dead drop: a corrupted data packet from Echo's early days.

I need a fresh pair of eyes. Someone who can look at this without preconceptions. I've uploaded the files. Help me piece this together. I'll be checking in with questions. Follow the clues, and together we can expose them.

· A. Finch"

The Core Mystery

You, the player, are assisting Hunter Finch. The "Echo Corporation" used sophisticated cryptographic techniques to hide their early propaganda plans. Your job is to decrypt their files and uncover the blueprint of their first major disinformation campaign.

Level 1: The Encrypted Directive +

File Received: ENCRYPTED_DIRECTIVE.txt (Appears as gibberish)

Hunter's Question #1 (Audio Log): "Play the audio file briefing.wav. My informant said the key to deciphering the text is hidden in my own words. What is the passphrase I mention that seems out of place?"

Your Task:

Listen to the audio file and identify the passphrase.

Level 2: The Forged Document +

File Unlocked: candidate_profile.jpg

Hunter's Question #2 (Text Message): "Good work. Now, analyze the image file. My tech expert says propaganda firms often hide clues in the data of the images themselves, not just what you see. Can you find the hidden message in the picture's metadata? What is the name of the original author?"

Your Task:

Examine the image metadata to find the author name.

Level 3: The Distribution List +

File Unlocked: distribution_list.dat (A list of scrambled website URLs)

Hunter's Question #3 (Video Call Simulator): "This list is encoded. We believe Echo used a simple cipher based on the keyboard. Each letter is shifted to the one immediately to the left on a standard QWERTY keyboard. Can you decipher the first URL on the list? That will be their primary target."

Your Task:

Decode the first URL: Rppm://kvsb.ibs/echo_target

Level 4: The Blueprint - Connecting the Dots +

File Unlocked: final_puzzle.zip (Password Protected)

Hunter's Final Question: "This is it. The master plan. The password is a combination of the clues you've found. Use the passphrase from my briefing, the author's alias, and the target website. Combine them into a single key. What is the password?"

Your Task:

Combine the clues to create the password.
